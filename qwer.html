Rules for meeting the British Queen (now King Charles III) involve strict etiquette: do not touch them, address them as "Your Majesty" then "Ma'am," bow or curtsy, and wait for them to initiate conversation. Key protocols include not taking selfies, avoiding shellfish to prevent food poisoning, and following the monarch’s lead in eating or leaving. [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6] Key Etiquette When Meeting the Queen (Monarch)
• Greeting: A neck bow (men) or small curtsy (women) is traditional, though a handshake is acceptable if offered.
• Physical Contact: Do not touch the Queen. Hugs or hand-on-back gestures are forbidden; only shake hands if she extends hers first.
• Addressing: The first address is "Your Majesty" and subsequently "Ma'am" (pronounced with a short 'a', as in "jam").
• Conversation & Dining: Let the Queen initiate conversation. Do not eat until she starts, and do not leave until she does.
• Protocol: Arrive before the royal guest. Avoid personal questions. [1, 2, 4, 6, 7, 8]
Rules of the Royal Household (Etiquette/Traditions)
• No Selfies/Autographs: Unofficial photos and autographs are discouraged; Queen Elizabeth II's handbag (https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=P9NaB_-cEKo) was used to signal staff to move on.
• Tiaras: Tiaras are for married women only, worn after 6 p.m..
• Clothing: No bare shoulders in church, and neutral nail polish is expected.
• Gifts: Family gifts are exchanged on Christmas Eve (tea time) rather than Christmas Day.
• Marriage: The first six in line to the throne require the sovereign's consent to marry, according to the Wikipedia article on succession (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Succession_to_the_British_throne). [3, 5, 9]
Constitutional Roles (Monarch)
• Parliament: The sovereign formally summons, prorogues (ends a session), and dissolves Parliament.
• State Visits: The monarch uses clothing and jewelry for "fashion diplomacy," often wearing local colors or specific brooches while traveling.
• Appointments: The sovereign formally appoints the Prime Minister and other ministers, says Wikipedia (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom).
